Legal & Policy

Terms of Data Use, Access & Retrieval

How CLNIQ handles your clinical data — with clarity, compliance, and full transparency.

Version 0.1  ·  Effective upon onboarding  ·  Governed under DPDP Act 2023 & NMC Guidelines

1Ownership of Data

CLNIQ operates on a "Zero-Ownership" policy regarding clinical information. Data ownership is clearly defined across three categories:

  • Clinical Data
    All medical records, prescriptions, and diagnostic reports belong to the Patient, managed by the Physician.
  • Administrative Data
    Clinic-specific scheduling and billing data belong to the Physician / Clinic.
  • Platform Data
    CLNIQ owns the software architecture, anonymized system-wide performance metrics, and the platform's user interface.

2Permitted Use of Data

Data processed by CLNIQ is used strictly for the following purposes:

  • Direct Care
    To enable the Physician to provide medical consultation and treatment.
  • System Operations
    To provide automated appointment reminders, billing generation, and technical support.
  • Legal Compliance
    To maintain records for the mandatory 3-year period as required by the NMC.
  • Prohibited Use
    CLNIQ will never use personal health data for third-party marketing, profiling, or sale to insurance companies without explicit, separate consent.

3Data Access — Who Can See What?

Access is governed by the principle of "Least Privilege" — users only see what is necessary for their role.

  • Physicians & Staff
    Access is granted via Role-Based Access Control (RBAC). A billing clerk cannot see clinical notes, and a lab technician cannot see the physician's private summaries.
  • Patients
    Patients have the right to access their fully digital medical record through the Physician's assistance upon demand on the CLNIQ Patient Portal.
  • CLNIQ Administrators
    Our team has no access to decrypted clinical data. Technical access is limited to metadata (e.g., system logs) for troubleshooting purposes only.

4Data Retrieval & Portability

We believe your data should be mobile. CLNIQ provides straightforward pathways for data retrieval — the "No Lock-In" Guarantee.

  • On-Demand Export
    Physicians can export their clinic's data at any time in industry-standard formats including CSV, JSON, or PDF.
  • Data Portability
    Patients can request a digital copy of their records to be transferred to another healthcare provider or to their personal health locker (ABHA).
  • Retrieval After Termination
    If a Physician ends their subscription, CLNIQ provides a 30-day grace period during which all clinical data can be retrieved before the account transitions to "Cold Storage" or is deleted.

5Data Deletion — Right to be Forgotten

  • Patient Requests
    A patient may request the deletion of their data. However, CLNIQ and the Physician must balance this against the NMC's 3-year mandatory retention rule for medical records.
  • Permanent Erasure
    Once the legal retention period expires or the purpose of processing is complete, CLNIQ uses industry-standard "Digital Shredding" to ensure the data cannot be recovered from Microsoft Azure storage.

Data Lifecycle Summary

Stage Action Responsibility
Collection Patient provides info to Physician via CLNIQ. Physician (Data Fiduciary)
Storage Encrypted data sits on Azure India servers. CLNIQ (Data Processor)
Access Physician views record to provide care. Physician (via RBAC)
Retrieval Clinic exports data for backup or migration. CLNIQ (Self-service tools)
Deletion Data is erased after the 3-year legal limit. CLNIQ (Automatic / Manual)

Transparency Note

CLNIQ maintains an "Audit Trail" of every instance where data is accessed or retrieved. Both the Physician and the Patient can request an Access Log to see who viewed their records and when.
